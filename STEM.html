STEM

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics

The STEM strand is designed for students who are interested in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. It helps develop critical thinking, problem-solving, and analytical skills needed to understand and create innovations.

Students study subjects such as biology, chemistry, physics, and advanced mathematics. These subjects build a strong foundation in scientific knowledge and improve logical thinking and data analysis skills.

Students also participate in hands-on activities like experiments, research projects, and group work. These activities help them apply what they learn and develop practical and technical skills.

The STEM strand prepares students for college courses in engineering, medicine, information technology, architecture, and other science-related fields. It is ideal for those who enjoy discovering how things work and solving real-world problems.

Career Paths

Students who take STEM can pursue careers such as engineer, doctor, nurse, architect, scientist, IT specialist, programmer, or pilot, as well as opportunities in fields like robotics and environmental science.
