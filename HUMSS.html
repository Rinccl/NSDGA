HUMSS

Humanities and Social Sciences

The HUMSS strand is designed for students who are interested in understanding people, society, and culture. It helps develop communication, critical thinking, and analytical skills, which are important in expressing ideas and understanding different perspectives in everyday life.

Students study subjects such as literature, philosophy, political science, and other social sciences. These subjects allow them to explore human behavior, social issues, history, and how different institutions shape society.

Through activities like essay writing, group discussions, debates, and presentations, students improve their confidence in speaking and expressing their thoughts. They also learn how to analyze information and form logical, well-supported opinions.

The HUMSS strand prepares students for college courses related to communication, education, law, psychology, and other social science fields. It is ideal for those who enjoy reading, writing, public speaking, and understanding social issues.

Career Paths

Students who take HUMSS can pursue careers such as teacher, lawyer, journalist, psychologist, social worker, public relations officer, or government employee.
